Model B: Error Correction Model (ECM) — Methodology ▸
ModelError Correction Model — price deviation from Model A fair value
Spread(Close Price − Fair Value Base) / Fair Value Base × 100
Z-Score Window60-day rolling (population std dev, min 30 observations)
Half-LifeEstimated days to mean-revert: 60 / (1 + |z|)
Signal Logicz < -1.5 → STRONG_REVERT_UP · z < -0.5 → MILD_REVERT_UP · |z| ≤ 0.5 → NEUTRAL · z > 0.5 → MILD_EXTEND_DOWN · z > 1.5 → STRONG_EXTEND_DOWN
Concept: When price deviates far from fair value, a correcting force pulls it back. Large negative z-scores = undervalued, likely to revert upward.
Model C: Multi-Factor Momentum — Methodology ▸
ModelComposite z-score from 4 exchange-level activity factors
Factor 1Daily Revenue z-score (weight: 40%) — MCX F&O transaction revenue
Factor 2Turnover z-score (weight: 25%) — futures notional + options premium
Factor 3Share Volume z-score (weight: 20%) — MCX share trading volume
Factor 4Inverse Intraday Volatility z-score (weight: 15%) — lower intraday range = bullish
Z-Score Window60-day rolling (population std dev, min 30 observations)

Rising revenue + turnover + volume with low intraday price volatility = bullish exchange activity momentum for MCX stock.
